What 3QP Is

3QP is a causal, deterministic behavioral simulation engine for small isolated crews. It is designed to model, reproduce, and study the Third-Quarter Phenomenon (TQP) — the empirically documented psychological and social deterioration that occurs roughly 50–75% through long-duration confined missions such as MARS-500, HI-SEAS, and Concordia.

The name refers to the phenomenon itself. The goal is a mission twin — a digital replica of a specific crew that runs day-by-day alongside a real mission and tracks psychological health at the individual level. A Mission Control operator can inject communications into the twin at any time and observe the projected effect before acting in the real world.

Core Design Principles

  • Causal, not statistical. Every number traces through an auditable chain: resource → perception → belief → internal state → action → physics. No regression, no ML, no hidden state.
  • Deterministic. The same crew, the same events, and the same seed always produce exactly the same trajectory. This enables true counterfactuals: “what would have happened if Mission Control sent a check-in on day 100?”
  • Layered and separated. The physics engine cannot be written to by behavioral layers. Narrative rendering cannot influence simulation outcomes. These invariants are enforced structurally.
  • Per-agent, personality-grounded. Each astronaut has a Big Five (OCEAN) personality profile that modulates every stage of the pipeline — how they perceive scarcity, how fast their beliefs shift, and when they withdraw vs. cooperate.
  • Transparent and auditable. Every simulation day is serialized to JSON. Every intermediate state — resources, perceptions, beliefs, internal state, actions, physics inputs, physics outputs — is logged and inspectable.

What 3QP Is Not

  • Not a scheduling or logistics tool
  • Not an AI or ML system — no training, no weights, no inference
  • Not a full BDI agent architecture — it is a minimal causal dynamical model
  • Not yet validated against real analog mission data (calibration pipeline is ready; data is pending)

What Is Measured & How

Every simulation day produces a complete snapshot. There are four measurement families, each corresponding to a layer in the architecture.

A. Physics Layer — 4 Crew-Level State Variables

The RuthlessCoreModel is the frozen physics engine. It takes four scalar inputs each day and updates four state variables via discrete-time difference equations. These are the primary outputs used for mission health assessment.

M Monotony
Accumulates boredom and environmental sameness. Knocked back by novelty events.
M[t+1] = max(0, M[t] + m_base − m_novelty × novelty)

m_base   = 0.008  (daily increment)
m_novelty = 0.20  (reduction per novelty event)
S Strain
Accumulated psychological load. Rises from workload and monotony; leaks via natural adaptation.
S[t+1] = max(0, S[t]
  + s_workload × workload
  + s_mono    × M[t]
  − s_recovery × recovery
  − s_leak    × S[t])

s_workload = 0.03, s_recovery = 0.04
s_mono = 0.004, s_leak = 0.030
Q Third-Quarter Pressure
Endogenous — emerges from M, S, and C. Not clock-based. TQP timing is a result, not an input.
Q[t+1] = clamp(
  c_q_m × (M/2.0)        # normalized monotony
+ c_q_s × S              # strain
+ c_q_c × max(0, 1−C))   # cohesion deficit

c_q_m = 0.15, c_q_s = 0.20, c_q_c = 0.10
C Cohesion
Social bond quality. The primary TQP indicator. Bounded [0.05, 1.2]. Includes a rebound mechanism.
C[t+1] = clamp(C[t]
  − c_strain × S[t]       # strain erodes
  − c_q      × Q[t]       # TQ pressure erodes
  + c_success × success   # shared wins restore
  + rebound)              # if Q actively falling

rebound = c_rebound × (1−C[t])  if Q[t+1] < Q[t]
Why Q is endogenous: The original model used a calendar-based Gaussian peak at mission midpoint. That was replaced. Q now emerges when — and only when — the crew's actual monotony, strain, and cohesion align to create pressure. A well-resourced, well-supported crew can complete 200 days without a TQP event. A fragile crew can trigger it early.

B. Per-Agent Layer — 7 Internal State Variables (per astronaut)

Each of the four crew members has an independent state updated daily from their personal beliefs and perceptions.

VariableRangeWhat It MeasuresPrimary Drivers
stress[0,1]Psychological loadScarcity belief, MC neglect, unfairness — buffered by morale
morale[0,1]Motivation and positive affectMission viability belief, MC support belief, crew cohesion belief
fatigue[0,1]Physical/mental exhaustionAccumulated stress; recovered by sleep quality
boredom[0,1]Mission disengagementLow viability belief; reduced by crew cohesion
trust_in_crew[0,1]Felt trust toward crewmatesSocial network graph mean edge weight
frustration_scarcity[0,1]Resource grievance levelScarcity + unfairness beliefs; relieved by MC support
future_outlook[0,1]Hope vs. resignationDirect proxy of mission viability belief
cooperation_threshold[0,1]Derived: friction to cooperative action0.45×stress + 0.25×fatigue + 0.30×frustration
Cooperation gate: If cooperation_threshold ≥ 0.70, the agent is blocked from cooperative actions regardless of intent. SUPPORT downgrades to MAINTAIN; ENGAGE downgrades to WITHDRAW. The astronaut wants to cooperate but is physically and psychologically incapacitated.

These are the ground truth — what is physically available aboard the habitat. Agents never read these directly; they go through the perception layer first.

D. Social Network Layer — Full Graph Metrics (Module 05)

Module 05 (SocialNetworkModule) maintains a full weighted undirected graph over the crew. Every day, each agent broadcasts interaction signals to every crewmate with intensity determined by their action type. The graph updates via tie-strength drift, passive decay, and active strengthening. Six metrics are captured per day:

MetricMeaning
global_cohesionMean edge weight across all crew pairs — overall social bond health
fragmentation_indexDegree of social splintering — high = cliques isolating from each other
normalized_densityFraction of possible connections still maintained at meaningful strength
clique_countNumber of detected subgroups (Bron-Kerbosch algorithm)
clique_coverageFraction of crew belonging to at least one clique
component_countNumber of disconnected components — approaching 2 signals dyadic fracture

Action-to-signal intensity mapping (drives daily graph updates):

SUPPORT  → 0.80   (cooperative, high-contact)
ENGAGE   → 0.60   (active, medium-positive)
MAINTAIN → 0.30   (neutral, low contact)
ESCALATE → 0.15   (conflict-adjacent)
WITHDRAW → 0.10   (avoidance, minimal contact)

Micro-event modifiers (crew-wide):
  conflict → −0.15   success → +0.10   novel → +0.05

E. Micro-Events — Daily Stochastic Perturbations

Approximately 52% of days have a micro-event drawn from a seeded RNG (deterministic). Events perturb task load or sleep quality for that day only; effects route through the resource layer and then through the full perception → belief → state pipeline.

Phase-aware event distribution: Event probabilities shift by mission phase. In the third-quarter window (50–74%): conflict events scale ×1.8, schedule slips ×1.3, novel tasks ×0.5, small wins ×0.6. In late phase (75–100%): slips ×1.5, task failures ×1.4, novelty ×0.3, wins ×0.4. This reflects real mission environmental change (novelty decay, task repetition) without forcing agent state directly — all causal paths still run through perception → belief → state.

How Agents Have Behavior

Agents are not scripted. Their behavior emerges because every output they produce is a deterministic function of their current internal state, which is itself a function of their subjective beliefs, which are a function of their personality-filtered perception of objective reality. The chain runs in full every day for every agent.

The critical design invariant: no state variable has a time-indexed drift term. An agent's stress does not rise simply because it is day 150. It rises because their beliefs about resource scarcity, MC support, and fairness have deteriorated — and those beliefs deteriorated because their perceptions have been negative for many days.

Step 1 — Perception: What the Agent Thinks They See

The PerceptionEngine takes the objective ResourceState and produces a subjective PerceivedState — seven filtered readings. Each is modulated by the agent's Big Five personality traits:

PerceptionFormulaTraitEffect
Coffee scarcity (1 − stock) × (1 + (N−0.5) × 0.6) N Neurotic agents perceive scarcity as 30% more severe at peak neuroticism
Food quality food_variety × (1 + (O−0.5) × 0.4) O Open agents enjoy variety 20% more; closed agents tolerate it 20% less
Distribution fairness Share deviation × neuroticism (if underprivileged); × agreeableness (if over) N A Neurotic agents amplify grievance; agreeable agents tolerate overallocation with guilt
Mission support No comms: drifts toward env quality; Comms received: type-specific boost (+0.05–+0.15) A N Agreeableness raises the floor; neuroticism slows recovery without direct confirmation
Social tension (1 − mean_trust) × (1 + (E−0.5) × 0.5) E Extraverted agents feel interpersonal friction 25% more intensely at peak extraversion
Sleep quality sleep × (1 − (N−0.3) × 0.3) N Neuroticism reduces perceived sleep quality; stable agents sleep the same as the environment

Step 2 — Belief Update: What the Agent Now Believes

The BeliefUpdateEngine takes PerceivedState and produces AstronautBeliefState — six named beliefs that update slowly via inertial convergence: 

new_belief = prior × inertia + perception × (1 − inertia)

inertia = base_inertia + inertia_delta

inertia_delta = −(neuroticism − 0.5) × N_factor    # high N → less inertia → more reactive
             + (conscientiousness − 0.5) × C_factor  # high C → more inertia → requires evidence
BeliefTargetSpecial Logic
belief_coffee_scarcityperceived_coffee_scarcity—
belief_distribution_fairnessperceived_distribution_fairness—
belief_resupply_reliabilityperceived_comms_reliability+0.15 when resupply physically arrives; −0.05/overdue day when promise is broken
belief_mission_control_supportperceived_mission_supportAlso decays when resupply is overdue — a broken promise erodes institutional trust
belief_crew_cohesion1.0 − perceived_social_tensionInverted: high tension → low cohesion belief
belief_mission_viabilityComposite of all other beliefs(1−scarcity)×W₁ + fairness×W₂ + mc_support×W₃ + cohesion×W₄
Key property: An agent can remain hopeful (belief_mission_viability high) under scarcity if distribution is believed fair, MC is believed present, and crew cohesion is believed strong. Scarcity alone does not produce despair. The full belief environment matters.

Step 3 — Internal State Drift: How the Agent Feels

The StateDriftEngine updates 7 internal variables from beliefs and perceptions. No inertia here — deltas are applied additively, then blended. Key equations:

Stress

target = belief_coffee_scarcity    × 0.060
       + (1 − belief_mc_support)   × 0.030
       + (1 − belief_fairness)     × 0.040
       − morale                    × 0.025    # morale buffers stress

target × neg_amplifier  # neg_amplifier = 1 + (N−0.5)×0.20
stress = prior×0.80 + target×0.20

Morale

delta = belief_mission_viability × 0.050
      + belief_mc_support          × 0.025
      + belief_crew_cohesion       × 0.025
      − stress                     × 0.030

delta × pos_buffer   # pos_buffer = 1 + (A−0.5)×0.15
morale = prior×0.82 + (prior + delta×pos_buffer)×0.18

Fatigue

fatigue += stress × 0.045 − sleep_quality × 0.055   # no personality modulation (physiological)

Boredom

target = (1 − belief_mission_viability) × 0.040    # loss of purpose
       + belief_coffee_scarcity          × 0.015    # monotony without novelty
       − belief_crew_cohesion            × 0.050    # social engagement fights boredom
       − stress                          × 0.025    # anxiety ≠ boredom

Trust in Crew

trust = prior×0.80 + social_net_mean_edge_weight×0.20
floor  = agreeableness × 0.25   # agreeable agents maintain baseline trust
trust  = max(trust, floor)

Frustration

target = belief_coffee_scarcity        × 0.055
       + (1 − belief_fairness)          × 0.065    # unfairness is primary driver
       − belief_mc_support              × 0.035    # MC presence relieves it
       − belief_resupply_reliability    × 0.018

target × neg_amplifier   # same neuroticism amplifier as stress

Cooperation Threshold (derived, not drifted)

raw        = stress × 0.45 + fatigue × 0.25 + frustration × 0.30
cooperation_threshold = raw / pos_buffer   # agreeableness lowers the gate
                                            # threshold ≥ 0.70 blocks cooperative actions

Step 4 — Action Selection: What the Agent Does

The PerAgentSelector maps internal state to one of five actions using a personality-adjusted IntentPolicy. Internal state variables are first mapped to a synthetic representation compatible with the policy engine: 

synthetic.strain           = internal.stress
synthetic.cohesion         = internal.trust_in_crew
synthetic.monotony         = internal.boredom
synthetic.tq_pressure      = (1.0 − future_outlook) × 0.5
synthetic.mission_progress = day / mission_length

The five actions and what they mean:

ActionMeaningSocial SignalPhysics Effect
SUPPORTActively assist crewmates0.80 intensity+success_boost per fraction of crew in SUPPORT
ENGAGEInvest in mission task; fight monotony0.60 intensity+novelty_boost per fraction of crew in ENGAGE
MAINTAINHold steady; low cost, neutral0.30 intensityNominal workload and recovery modifiers
ESCALATEExpress conflict; signal fracture0.15 intensityElevated workload, reduced recovery
WITHDRAWDisengage entirely; avoidance0.10 intensityLow workload but recovery also falls

Personality modulates the thresholds that trigger each action:

TraitEffect on Thresholds
N Neuroticism Lowers withdraw and high-strain thresholds by up to −0.30. Reactive agents retreat and escalate sooner.
A Agreeableness Lowers support threshold (−0.10); raises escalation threshold (+0.15). Cooperative agents offer help earlier and fight less.
E Extraversion Lowers monotony/engage threshold (−0.20). Social agents engage more readily when bored.
C Conscientiousness Raises support threshold (+0.10). Deliberate agents are more selective about when to offer help.

After the policy selects an action, the cooperation gate fires: if cooperation_threshold ≥ 0.70, SUPPORT → MAINTAIN and ENGAGE → WITHDRAW. The agent's metadata records the gate firing, original intent, and threshold value.

Step 5 — Physics Aggregation: How Individual Behavior Drives Collective Dynamics

aggregate_crew_inputs() folds all four agents' actions into four physics modifiers that scale the RuthlessCoreModel inputs for that day: 

workload_modifier = mean(workload_multiplier per action)
recovery_modifier = mean(recovery_multiplier per action)
novelty_boost     = (fraction in ENGAGE) × engage_novelty_coefficient
success_boost     = (fraction in SUPPORT) × support_success_coefficient

effective_workload = base_workload × workload_modifier
effective_recovery = base_recovery × recovery_modifier

# Executive function degradation: chronic fatigue+stress degrade coordination
# Activates above ~0.35 combined impairment; up to +25% workload, -20% recovery
exec_impairment    = clamp((mean_fatigue×0.55 + mean_stress×0.45 − 0.35) × 1.4)
effective_workload = clamp(effective_workload × (1 + exec_impairment × 0.25))
effective_recovery = clamp(effective_recovery × (1 − exec_impairment × 0.20))

# Structural competence: high fatigue+boredom reduces quality of collective effort
effort_quality     = clamp(1 − max(0, (mean_burnout − 0.30)) × 1.3)
novelty_boost     *= effort_quality   # ENGAGE less energizing when burned out
success_boost     *= effort_quality   # SUPPORT less effective when exhausted

effective_novelty  = base_novelty  + novelty_boost
effective_success  = base_success  + success_boost
Individual behavior shapes collective physics. A crew where 3 of 4 agents are withdrawing produces lower novelty_boost (boredom accelerates), lower success_boost (cohesion recovery weakens), and lower social signal intensity (graph edges decay). These compound. The cascade is fully auditable — every day's JSON records which agent took which action, what gate fired, and what physics inputs resulted.

The Closed Loop — How the System Propagates

Actions today → social network update → edge weights change
                                        ↓
                             tomorrow's SocialProximityMap
                                        ↓
                             tomorrow's perceived_social_tension
                                        ↓
                             belief_crew_cohesion degrades
                                        ↓
                             morale falls → stress rises → cooperation_threshold rises
                                        ↓
                             cooperative actions blocked → ENGAGE→WITHDRAW, SUPPORT→MAINTAIN
                                        ↓
                             weak social signals → edges decay further
                                        ↓
                             (cycle repeats and amplifies)

This is the cascade pattern. An astronaut who withdraws reduces their interaction signal intensity. Social edges weaken. Their crewmate perceives higher tension. The crewmate believes crew cohesion is falling. The crewmate's morale drops. The crewmate's cooperation threshold rises. The crewmate also withdraws. The loop is neither programmed nor scripted — it emerges from the physics of the system.

Personality → Physics: How Big Five Maps to RuthlessCoreConfig

When a crew preset is loaded, PersonalityToConfigMapper computes each crew member's Big Five aggregate and maps it to the physics parameters that govern that crew's trajectory. This means the same scenario (same events, same resources) produces genuinely different outcomes for different personality compositions:

TraitPhysics ParameterFormulaInterpretation
N Neuroticism initial_strain 0.0 + N × 0.15 Neurotic crews start with pre-existing stress
q_peak 0.50 + N × 0.15 Neurotic crews develop stronger TQ pressure
C Conscientiousness s_workload 0.035 − C × 0.015 Conscientious crews handle workload more efficiently (less strain per unit)
c_rebound 0.008 + C × 0.007 Conscientious crews recover cohesion better when pressure eases
A Agreeableness c_strain 0.012 − A × 0.007 Agreeable crews' cohesion is less damaged by per-agent strain
c_q 0.040 − A × 0.015 Agreeable crews' cohesion is more resilient to TQ pressure
E Extraversion m_base 0.006 + E × 0.005 Extraverted crews are more sensitive to isolation and sameness

O Openness m_novelty (multiplier), c_shared_success m_novelty × (1 + O×0.2); 0.05 + O×0.03 Open crews get more novelty benefit and are more energized by shared wins

Mission Control Communications — The Intervention Pathway

An MCCommunication is injected into the runner externally by a human operator. It travels through the pipeline with a one-day delivery lag (dispatched today, received tomorrow): 

MCCommunication dispatch
        ↓  (next day)
PerceptionEngine reads MCCommSignal
  → perceived_comms_reliability += 0.10 (bonus for any received comm)
  → perceived_mission_support   += type-specific boost

BeliefUpdateEngine reads new perception
  → belief_mission_control_support converges toward new perceived support
  → belief_resupply_reliability   : +0.10 if resupply_promised

_apply_comm_deltas() applies direct belief deltas immediately:
  → belief_mc_support += comm.belief_mc_support_delta
  → belief_resupply   += comm.belief_resupply_reliability_delta
Comm Typemc_support_deltaresupply_reliability_deltaTypical use
support+0.100Active emotional support message
reassurance+0.080Psychological check-in, mission confidence
acknowledgment+0.060MC recognizes crew effort
direction+0.050Task guidance, operational clarity
resupply_announcement+0.04+0.10Resupply en route — double benefit
Broken promises decay trust. If a resupply is announced but arrives late, both belief_resupply_reliability and belief_mission_control_support decay by ~0.05 per overdue day. A broken MC promise is worse than no promise at all.
Intervention compliance scales with crew state. High-strain and high-frustration agents are less receptive to MC communications. Belief deltas are scaled by a compliance factor: compliance = max(0.35, 1 − strain_reactance − frustration_reactance) where each reactance term activates above 0.5. At peak strain and frustration, an intervention delivers only 35% of its nominal belief delta. Interventions are never inert, but they are significantly dampened where they are needed most — early intervention matters.
