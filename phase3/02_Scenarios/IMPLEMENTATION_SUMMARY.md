# Implementation Summary: Scenario Definition

## Overview

This document summarizes the creation of Phase 3, Workstream 2 documentation for Scenario Definition. All files adhere strictly to the requirement for purely qualitative, conceptual, non-numeric content that describes how mission scenarios unfold through events, state transitions, and narrative arcs.

---

## Files Created

### 1. SCENARIO_TEMPLATES.md

**Purpose**: Define three qualitative scenario templates describing different mission arcs.

**Content**:

**A. Nominal Mission Scenario**
- Stable, well-functioning mission progression
- Starting conditions from baseline state
- Event progression through qualitative phases: early mission, transition to mid-mission, mid-mission stability, late mission, conclusion
- Dominant themes: routine maintenance, gradual adaptation, social stability, manageable monotony, sustained competence
- Expected adaptations: routine refinement, social calibration, environmental familiarity, role mastery
- Trajectory descriptions across physiological, cognitive, social, coping, and workload domains

**B. Stressor-Driven Disruption Scenario**
- Mission arc encountering escalating pressures and challenges
- Event progression: baseline to emergence, escalation, intensification, critical phase, resolution/stabilization, recovery or new equilibrium
- Dominant themes: compounding demands, resource depletion, uncertainty, social friction, resilience testing
- Expected adaptations and frictions: creative problem-solving, social mobilization vs. interpersonal conflict, withdrawal, cognitive narrowing
- Trajectory descriptions showing degradation under stress and potential recovery patterns

**C. Third-Quarter Phenomenon Scenario**
- Mid-mission psychological and social drift pattern
- Event progression: engagement and novelty, settling in, mid-mission drift, deepening phase, inflection point, recovery or sustained drift, late mission re-engagement
- Dominant themes: temporal ambiguity, cumulative monotony, motivational erosion, social saturation, psychological endurance
- Expected adaptations and frictions: conscious re-engagement, variety creation vs. passive drift, social withdrawal
- Trajectory descriptions capturing gradual erosion and potential recovery through renewed purpose

**Approach**: Purely narrative descriptions of mission arcs using qualitative phases and conceptual language without any time steps, numeric durations, or computational elements.

### 2. EVENT_ARCS.md

**Purpose**: Define the qualitative structure of events that shape mission scenarios.

**Content**:

**Event Types**:
- Operational events (equipment, procedures, tasks, communication)
- Environmental events (habitat conditions, external environment, resources)
- Social events (interpersonal dynamics, relationships, group processes)
- Cognitive/intentional events (information processing, decision-making, belief formation)
- Emotional/interpersonal events (mood shifts, psychological experiences)

**Event Phases**:
- Emergence (early indicators, potential for prevention)
- Escalation (intensification, increasing demands)
- Stabilization (plateau, managed but not resolved)
- Resolution (conclusion, problem solved or accepted)
- After-effects (residual impacts, recovery processes)

**Event Impact Patterns** (qualitative descriptions of how events influence):
- Stressor environment (acute vs. chronic pressure, stressor removal, modulation)
- Role expectations (expansion, ambiguity, conflict, clarification)
- Cognitive focus (narrowing, fragmentation, redirection, sharpening)
- Emotional tone (elevation, depression, anxiety, flattening, volatility)
- Crew cohesion (strengthening, weakening, polarization, reconnection)
- Coping resources (depletion, restoration, expansion, chronic drain)
- Workload interpretation (amplification, redistribution, reframing, clarification)

**Event Interaction Rules** (conceptual only):
- Compounding (multiple events amplify total impact)
- Reinforcement (similar events strengthen patterns)
- Neutralization (opposing events mitigate each other)
- Transformation (context changes event meaning)
- Cascading (one event triggers downstream events)
- Buffering (positive conditions reduce negative impacts)
- Sensitization (negative events increase vulnerability)
- Temporal interaction (timing, spacing, duration effects)

**Approach**: Conceptual framework for understanding event dynamics without numeric weights, probability, or computational rules.

### 3. EXPECTED_STATE_TRANSITIONS.md

**Purpose**: Define qualitative patterns of state change across all crew functioning domains.

**Content**:

**Physiological Transitions**:
- Stable functioning → Emerging strain → Degraded resilience → Severe exhaustion
- Recovery transitions in reverse direction
- Qualitative descriptions of sleep, fatigue, stress response, physical capacity changes

**Cognitive Transitions**:
- Clear focus → Divided priorities → Disrupted intention clarity → Cognitive fragmentation
- Recovery transitions through reintegration to restored clarity
- Descriptions of attention, decision-making, belief coherence, situational awareness changes

**Emotional Transitions**:
- Stable mood → Increased reactivity → Mood dysregulation → Emotional crisis
- Recovery transitions through stabilization to stable mood
- Descriptions of affect, emotional regulation, motivation, engagement changes

**Social Cohesion Transitions**:
- Cohesive → Strained → Polarized → Social fragmentation
- Recovery transitions through tentative reconnection to rebuilt cohesion
- Descriptions of communication quality, trust, cooperation, collective functioning changes

**Coping and Adaptive Capacity Transitions**:
- Adequate resources → Stretched capacity → Depleted reserves → Adaptive breakthrough or maladaptive collapse
- Recovery transitions through restoration to adequate resources
- Descriptions of resource availability, adaptive bandwidth, coping effectiveness changes

**Environmental and Workload Perception Transitions**:
- Routine → Variable → Demanding → Overwhelming
- Recovery transitions through stabilizing to routine
- Descriptions of how demands are experienced and interpreted

**Belief-Desire-Intention (BDI) Transitions**:
- Aligned beliefs/goals → Internal conflicts → Shifting priorities → Renewed alignment or fragmentation
- Descriptions of intentional coherence, goal clarity, belief-action consistency

**Approach**: Purely qualitative descriptions of state changes using natural language characterizations without numeric scales, thresholds, or measurement systems.
