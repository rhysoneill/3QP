### 2. TRAJECTORY_ARCHETYPES.md

**Purpose**: Documents eight major qualitative trajectory archetypes representing common mission arcs.

**Content**:

**A. Stable Adaptation Trajectory**  
Steady navigation of mission challenges with sustained coherence, resilience, and effective cross-domain functioning.

**B. Gradual Drift Trajectory**  
Slow, incremental erosion across domains—sleep quality, cognitive flexibility, emotional vitality, social engagement—without dramatic crisis.

**C. Disruption–Stabilization Trajectory**  
Movement through acute or chronic challenges into turbulence, followed by recovery or stabilization into new equilibrium.

**D. Third-Quarter Trajectory**  
Mid-mission psychological and relational erosion characterized by energy depletion, meaning loss, and a sense of being stuck in the endless middle.

**E. Cumulative Strain Trajectory**  
Progressive wear across all domains—physiological depletion, cognitive narrowing, emotional flatness, social fragmentation—leading to constrained endurance.

**F. Meaning Erosion Trajectory**  
Loss of connection to mission purpose and significance, with functioning that remains adequate but feels hollow and disconnected.

**G. Recovery–Renewal Trajectory**  
Movement from strain, depletion, or constraint back toward vitality, coherence, meaning, and relational depth.

**H. Divergent Crew Trajectory**  
Internal heterogeneity where crew members experience different arcs simultaneously, creating mismatched rhythms and complex relational dynamics.

**Alignment with 3QP Architecture**:
- Each archetype integrates physiology, cognition, emotion, social dynamics, and operational context
- All descriptions reference patterns, threads, and scenarios from prior workstreams
- Archetypes are presented as interpretive frameworks, not prescriptive templates
- Variation across individuals and missions is explicitly acknowledged

### 3. TRAJECTORY_COMPONENTS.md

**Purpose**: Defines the conceptual building blocks that constitute qualitative trajectories.

**Content**:

**Trajectory Anchors**  
Recognizable points where crew state feels notably different—narrative markers that structure trajectory stories.

**Inflection Moments**  
Specific experiences where the trajectory's direction or quality shifts—the turning points that reshape mission arcs.

**Cross-Domain Cascades**  
How changes in one domain propagate into others, creating movement across the entire trajectory through multi-domain interaction.

**Stabilizing Influences**  
Elements that maintain or restore balance—rituals, relationships, rest, meaning, autonomy, environmental richness.

**Destabilizing Influences**  
Elements that contribute to drift or strain—isolation, sleep disruption, operational overload, relational tension, meaning loss, monotony.

**Trajectory Rhythms**  
Qualitative descriptions of pacing, tone, and movement—steady, turbulent, gradual, punctuated, stagnant, oscillating.

**Trajectory Signatures**  
Distinct experiential patterns that give trajectories recognizable identity and narrative coherence.

**Alignment with 3QP Architecture**:
- Components provide language for articulating how trajectories are structured and evolve
- All components are described qualitatively, without metrics or mechanisms
- Components integrate naturally with patterns, threads, scenarios, and baseline states
- Framework supports interpretation and sense-making rather than prediction or control

## Alignment with 3QP Architecture

### Integration with Prior Workstreams

**Workstream 1: Baseline States and Reference Patterns**  
Trajectories begin from baseline and move through reference states—stability, strain, recovery, constrained equilibrium. Trajectories describe the motion through these states rather than the states themselves.

**Workstream 2: Scenarios and Event Arcs**  
Trajectories are shaped by scenarios and events. Events punctuate trajectories; scenarios are embedded within trajectories. Trajectories provide the broader mission context that gives events and scenarios their meaning and consequence.

**Workstream 3: Cross-Domain Patterns**  
Trajectories describe movement through patterns. A trajectory might begin in high coherence, transition through cognitive narrowing, and stabilize in constrained equilibrium. Patterns are the configurations; trajectories are the journey.

**Workstream 4: Cross-Module Threads**  
Trajectories are woven from threads. The threads of sleep disruption, meaning erosion, trust drift, and cognitive narrowing combine to shape the trajectory's direction and tone. Threads describe domain-specific or cross-domain evolution; trajectories integrate all active threads into a single arc.

**Workstream 5: Qualitative Trajectories**  
Trajectories synthesize all prior workstreams into whole-mission narrative arcs. They are the integrative, highest-order structure in Phase 3—the complete story of how a crew experiences and navigates the demands of deep-space missions.

### Consistency with Phase 3 Principles

**Purely Qualitative**  
Every document in this workstream maintains strict qualitative compliance. No numbers, metrics, percentages, durations, thresholds, or mechanistic structures appear. All content is expressed in narrative, conceptual language.

**Multi-Domain Integration**  
Trajectories integrate physiology, cognition, emotion, social dynamics, and operational context into coherent, whole-person and whole-crew narratives.

**Interpretive, Not Predictive**  
Trajectories are frameworks for sense-making, not forecasting tools. They help crews and support teams recognize, anticipate, and interpret mission dynamics without claiming deterministic prediction.

**Context-Sensitive and Variable**  
Trajectories acknowledge that every mission is unique. Archetypes describe common patterns, but actual trajectories are shaped by mission design, crew composition, environmental factors, and unforeseen events.

**Human-Centered Language**  
All trajectory descriptions are written for human understanding and use. Language is accessible to crews, psychologists, trainers, and mission planners—not requiring technical or computational expertise.

## Completion of Phase 3 Conceptual Foundation

Workstream 5 completes the conceptual architecture of Phase 3. The five workstreams together provide a comprehensive, integrated, qualitative framework for understanding deep-space crew functioning:

1. **Baseline States and Reference Patterns**: The foundational configurations of functioning across domains
2. **Scenarios and Event Arcs**: The challenges, disruptions, and transitions that shape crew experience
3. **Cross-Domain Patterns**: The snapshot-like states that characterize crew functioning at any moment
4. **Cross-Module Threads**: The evolving dynamics within and across domains over the mission lifecycle
5. **Qualitative Trajectories**: The whole-mission narrative arcs that integrate all prior components into coherent stories

Together, these workstreams describe **what crews experience, how experience unfolds, and what shapes the arc of the mission**—all expressed in language that honors the complexity, variability, and profound humanity of deep-space exploration.

## Preparation for Phase 4

While Phase 3 maintains strict qualitative focus, it establishes the conceptual foundation that Phase 4 will translate into computational structures. Phase 4 will:

- Develop data representations for baseline states, patterns, threads, and trajectories
- Create computational methods for recognizing trajectory signatures and inflection moments
- Build systems for tracking cross-domain cascades and stabilizing/destabilizing influences
- Implement tools for crew self-assessment, mission planning, and psychological support

Phase 4 will perform this translation **without compromising the qualitative integrity** of Phase 3. The conceptual frameworks will remain intact; computational tools will serve the frameworks, not replace them.

The transition from Phase 3 to Phase 4 will be carefully managed to preserve the human-centered, interpretive character of the 3QP approach while enabling practical application in mission design, crew preparation, and real-time support.
