# Baseline State Specification

## Overview

This document defines the baseline state of the Three Question Protocol (3QP) system—the stable, equilibrium configuration from which all operational scenarios begin. The baseline represents a crew operating under normal conditions with no exceptional stressors, disruptions, or degraded states.

---

## Baseline Physiological State

### General Condition
The crew begins in a state of **stable physiological functioning**. Individuals are well-rested, adequately nourished, and free from cumulative strain. There is no sustained fatigue, no chronic arousal, and no elevated allostatic load.

### Sleep and Recovery
Sleep cycles are **regular and undisturbed**. Crew members experience predictable rest periods aligned with habitat scheduling. Recovery is sufficient to offset daily demands, maintaining a state of readiness.

### Stress Response Systems
The slow and fast physiological response systems are in a **balanced, homeostatic state**. There is no persistent activation of acute stress responses, nor is there a buildup of chronic strain. The body operates within normal adaptive ranges.

### Physical Capacity
Physical reserves are **adequate for routine operations**. There is no depletion, overexertion, or compromised functional capacity. The crew maintains normal performance capabilities.

---

## Baseline Stressor Environment

### Operational Demands
The environment presents **routine operational demands** typical of nominal mission phases. Tasks are predictable, well-understood, and manageable within established procedures and training.

### Workload Intensity
Workload is **balanced and distributed** across the crew. No individual or subsystem is overwhelmed. Demands remain within the range of what is considered normal for long-duration spaceflight.

### Environmental Pressures
Background pressures are **low-level and stable**. There are no acute emergencies, equipment failures, or life-threatening events. The habitat environment is functioning as designed.

### Temporal Characteristics
Stressors are **steady and predictable** rather than sudden or surprising. The crew operates with clear expectations about what each operational period will entail.

---

## Baseline Cognitive and BDI State

### Situational Awareness
Crew members have **clear and accurate understanding** of their environment, mission status, and operational context. There is no confusion, ambiguity, or information overload.

### Belief Structure
Beliefs about the mission, roles, and environment are **well-formed and stable**. Crew members hold accurate mental models of their situation and understand how their actions contribute to mission success.

### Desires and Goals
Individual and collective goals are **aligned with mission objectives**. Desires are coherent, prioritized, and mutually compatible. There are no competing demands or conflicting motivations.

### Intentions and Actions
Intentions are **clear and actionable**. Crew members know what they need to do, when to do it, and how to execute. Actions follow logically from beliefs and desires without interference or disruption.

### Decision-Making Capacity
Decision-making processes are **deliberate and effective**. Crew members can assess options, weigh consequences, and commit to courses of action without cognitive overload or paralysis.

---

## Baseline Coping Bandwidth

### Internal Resources
Crew members possess **adequate internal resources** to manage daily demands. This includes cognitive flexibility, emotional regulation, and problem-solving capacity.

### External Support
The crew has **access to necessary support systems**, including communication with mission control, functional equipment, and adequate supplies. Support structures are reliable and responsive.

### Workload Balance
The ratio of demands to available resources is **sustainable**. The crew is neither under-stimulated nor overwhelmed. There is sufficient capacity to absorb minor fluctuations without strain.

### Adaptive Capacity
The crew retains **reserve capacity** to respond to unexpected challenges. There is headroom for adaptation, learning, and improvisation when needed.

---

## Baseline Social Cohesion

### Interpersonal Relationships
Crew relationships are **cooperative and respectful**. Individuals communicate openly, trust one another, and collaborate effectively. There is no active conflict or relational breakdown.

### Shared Identity
The crew functions as a **cohesive unit** with a shared sense of purpose. Members identify with the team and the mission, reinforcing collective goals over individual interests.

### Communication Patterns
Communication is **clear, timely, and constructive**. Information flows freely among crew members. Misunderstandings are rare and quickly resolved.

### Social Support
Crew members provide **mutual support and encouragement**. There is a sense of camaraderie and connection. Individuals feel valued and understood by their peers.

### Fragmentation and Division
There is **no evidence of fragmentation**, cliques, or social isolation. The crew operates as an integrated whole without subgroup tensions or exclusion.

---

## Baseline Role Expectations and Workload Norms

### Role Clarity
Roles and responsibilities are **well-defined and understood**. Each crew member knows their function, their authority, and their boundaries. There is no role ambiguity or confusion.

### Workload Distribution
Work is **equitably distributed** according to training, expertise, and capacity. No individual is overburdened or underutilized. The division of labor is accepted as fair.

### Expectations and Accountability
Expectations are **mutually understood and realistic**. Crew members know what is expected of them and hold one another accountable in a constructive manner.

### Leadership and Authority
Leadership structures are **clear and functional**. Authority is exercised appropriately, and crew members respect the chain of command without resentment or resistance.

---

## Baseline Sleep and Fatigue Conditions

### Sleep Quality
Sleep is **restorative and sufficient**. Crew members fall asleep easily, sleep through scheduled rest periods, and wake feeling refreshed.

### Sleep Timing
Sleep schedules are **predictable and aligned** with mission operations. There is no chronic sleep deprivation, phase shifting, or circadian disruption.

### Fatigue Levels
Fatigue is **minimal and transient**. Any tiredness experienced is quickly relieved by rest. There is no accumulation of fatigue across days or weeks.

### Alertness
Crew members maintain **steady alertness** during operational periods. Attention, vigilance, and reaction times are within normal ranges.

---

## Cross-Module Baseline Summary

### Integrated Stable State
The baseline represents a state where **all subsystems operate in harmony**. Physiological systems are balanced, cognitive processes are clear, social dynamics are constructive, and environmental demands are manageable.

### Homeostatic Equilibrium
The crew exists in a **homeostatic equilibrium** where inputs and outputs, stressors and recovery, demands and resources are all balanced. There is no net accumulation of strain.

### Readiness for Operations
From this baseline, the crew is **fully prepared** to execute routine operations and capable of absorbing moderate perturbations without significant degradation.

### Starting Point for Scenarios
The baseline serves as the **reference configuration** against which all operational scenarios are measured. Deviations from baseline indicate the emergence of strain, adaptation, or breakdown.

### Absence of Exceptional Conditions
The baseline explicitly excludes **acute emergencies, equipment failures, interpersonal conflicts, sleep deprivation, chronic fatigue, cognitive overload, and social fragmentation**. These conditions represent departures from baseline.

---

## Summary

The baseline state is characterized by:
- **Stable physiology** with adequate rest and recovery
- **Routine operational demands** without acute stressors
- **Clear cognitive functioning** and aligned intentions
- **Adequate coping bandwidth** with balanced demands
- **Cohesive social dynamics** and mutual support
- **Well-defined roles** and equitable workload
- **Regular sleep cycles** and minimal fatigue

This is the foundational state from which the 3QP system begins all modeling and analysis.
