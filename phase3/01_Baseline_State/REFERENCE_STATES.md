# Reference States

## Overview

This document defines qualitative reference states across key dimensions of crew functioning. These states provide a descriptive vocabulary for characterizing crew condition without reliance on numeric measurement.

---

## Physiological Reference States

### Sleep and Rest States
- **Well-Rested**: Crew members have received adequate, restorative sleep over multiple cycles. Alertness is high, and fatigue is minimal.
- **Adequately Rested**: Sleep has been sufficient for basic recovery, though not ideal. Minor tiredness may be present but does not impair functioning.
- **Moderately Fatigued**: Sleep has been insufficient or disrupted. Alertness is reduced, and performance may show minor degradation.
- **Significantly Fatigued**: Chronic sleep deprivation or sustained wakefulness. Cognitive and physical performance are noticeably impaired.
- **Severely Fatigued**: Extreme sleep loss with serious impairment of judgment, reaction time, and physical capacity.

### Stress Response States
- **Calm and Balanced**: Physiological systems are in a stable, homeostatic state. No significant stress activation.
- **Mildly Aroused**: Slight activation of stress response systems in response to minor demands. Easily managed.
- **Moderately Strained**: Sustained activation of stress responses. Physiological systems are working to maintain equilibrium.
- **Highly Strained**: Prolonged or intense stress activation. Recovery capacity is diminished, and vulnerability to further strain increases.
- **Overwhelmed**: Physiological systems are unable to maintain balance. Breakdown or exhaustion is imminent.

### Physical Capacity States
- **Full Capacity**: Physical reserves are intact. Crew members can perform all required tasks without limitation.
- **Slightly Reduced Capacity**: Minor decrements in physical performance. Tasks can still be completed but with more effort.
- **Moderately Reduced Capacity**: Noticeable physical limitations. Some tasks may require assistance or take longer.
- **Significantly Reduced Capacity**: Major impairment of physical functioning. Many tasks are difficult or impossible without support.
- **Minimal Capacity**: Severe physical exhaustion or impairment. Crew member is largely incapacitated.

## Cognitive Reference States

### Mental Clarity States
- **Clear and Focused**: Thinking is sharp, organized, and efficient. Attention is sustained, and mental processes flow smoothly.
- **Generally Clear**: Cognitive functioning is adequate with occasional lapses in focus or minor distractions.
- **Somewhat Clouded**: Difficulty maintaining concentration. Thinking feels slower or less organized than usual.
- **Significantly Clouded**: Major difficulty with attention, memory, or reasoning. Mental effort feels labored.
- **Severely Impaired**: Profound cognitive dysfunction. Decision-making and problem-solving are deeply compromised.

### Decision-Making States
- **Confident and Decisive**: Decisions are made efficiently with clear rationale. Crew members feel capable and self-assured.
- **Deliberate and Cautious**: Decisions take more time but remain sound. Some uncertainty or second-guessing is present.
- **Hesitant**: Difficulty committing to decisions. Uncertainty and doubt interfere with timely action.
- **Paralyzed**: Unable to make decisions effectively. Overwhelmed by options or consequences.
- **Impulsive or Erratic**: Decisions are hasty, poorly reasoned, or inconsistent. Judgment is compromised.

### Belief and Understanding States
- **Clear Understanding**: Mental models are accurate and coherent. Situational awareness is strong.
- **Generally Accurate**: Understanding is mostly correct with minor gaps or ambiguities.
- **Partial Understanding**: Significant gaps or uncertainties in situational awareness. Confusion is present.
- **Confused**: Mental models are inaccurate or contradictory. Misunderstanding is common.
- **Disoriented**: Profound confusion about situation, context, or reality. Understanding has broken down.

## Emotional Reference States

### Mood and Affect States
- **Stable and Positive**: Mood is generally upbeat, calm, and consistent. Emotional responses are appropriate and proportional.
- **Neutral and Steady**: Mood is even without significant highs or lows. Emotional reactivity is low.
- **Mildly Negative**: Some irritability, sadness, or anxiety. Mood is slightly depressed but manageable.
- **Moderately Disturbed**: Noticeable emotional distress such as persistent anxiety, anger, or sadness.
- **Severely Disturbed**: Intense emotional turmoil. Affect is volatile, inappropriate, or overwhelming.

### Emotional Regulation States
- **Well-Regulated**: Emotions are experienced and expressed appropriately. Self-control is strong.
- **Generally Controlled**: Occasional emotional surges but overall regulation is maintained.
- **Somewhat Dysregulated**: Difficulty managing emotional responses. Outbursts or withdrawal may occur.
- **Poorly Regulated**: Frequent emotional instability. Reactions are disproportionate or unpredictable.
- **Unregulated**: Emotional chaos. Little to no capacity for self-control or modulation.

---

## Social Cohesion Reference States

### Crew Cohesion States
- **Highly Cohesive**: Strong sense of unity and shared purpose. Mutual trust and support are deeply embedded.
- **Cohesive**: Good teamwork and cooperation. Relationships are functional and positive.
- **Adequate Cohesion**: Basic cooperation is maintained, but connections are not particularly strong.
- **Developing Fragmentation**: Signs of tension or division. Subgroups or cliques may be forming.
- **Fragmented**: Crew is divided. Cooperation is minimal, and conflict is common.

### Communication States
- **Open and Constructive**: Communication is honest, clear, and supportive. Information flows freely.
- **Functional**: Communication serves its purpose but may lack depth or warmth.
- **Strained**: Communication is awkward, guarded, or tense. Misunderstandings are common.
- **Dysfunctional**: Communication is hostile, avoidant, or incoherent. Collaboration suffers.
- **Breakdown**: Communication has largely ceased or become counterproductive.

### Trust and Support States
- **High Trust**: Crew members rely on one another fully. Support is readily given and received.
- **Moderate Trust**: Trust exists but with some reservations. Support is available but not automatic.
- **Low Trust**: Suspicion or wariness is present. Support is conditional or withheld.
- **Distrust**: Active skepticism about others' motives or reliability. Support is minimal.
- **Hostile**: Trust has been betrayed. Relationships are adversarial.

## Workload and Coping Reference States

### Workload Perception States
- **Light and Manageable**: Demands feel well within capacity. There is time for rest and discretionary activities.
- **Moderate and Balanced**: Workload is substantial but sustainable. Demands match available resources.
- **Heavy but Tolerable**: Workload is challenging and requires sustained effort but remains achievable.
- **Overwhelming**: Demands exceed capacity. Crew members struggle to keep up.
- **Crushing**: Workload is so intense that breakdown or failure is imminent.

### Coping Resource States
- **Abundant Resources**: Strong internal and external resources. Crew feels capable and supported.
- **Adequate Resources**: Sufficient resources to meet demands with some reserve capacity.
- **Strained Resources**: Resources are stretched. Little margin for error or additional demands.
- **Depleted Resources**: Resources are nearly exhausted. Vulnerability is high.
- **Exhausted Resources**: Resources are gone. The crew is operating on fumes or beyond capacity.

### Adaptive Capacity States
- **Highly Adaptive**: Crew responds flexibly and creatively to challenges. Learning and adjustment occur readily.
- **Moderately Adaptive**: Crew can adapt but with effort and some trial and error.
- **Limited Adaptability**: Adaptation is slow or incomplete. Crew struggles with novelty or change.
- **Rigid**: Little capacity for adjustment. Crew clings to familiar patterns even when ineffective.
- **Maladaptive**: Responses worsen the situation. Adaptation has broken down entirely.

## Stressor Environment Reference States

### Operational Demand States
- **Routine Operations**: Predictable, well-understood tasks within normal parameters.
- **Elevated Demands**: Tasks are more complex, urgent, or numerous than usual but still manageable.
- **High-Intensity Operations**: Sustained high demands requiring focused effort and coordination.
- **Crisis Operations**: Acute emergency requiring immediate, high-stakes decision-making and action.
- **Catastrophic Events**: Life-threatening scenario with extreme demands and limited options.

### Environmental Pressure States
- **Low Background Pressure**: Stable habitat with minimal external stressors.
- **Moderate Pressure**: Some environmental challenges such as minor system alerts or schedule pressure.
- **Heightened Pressure**: Multiple sources of pressure such as equipment issues, time constraints, or resource concerns.
- **Severe Pressure**: Compounding environmental threats creating significant operational difficulty.
- **Extreme Pressure**: Cascading failures or converging threats creating existential danger.

### Temporal Characteristics of Stressors
- **Brief and Transient**: Stressors last moments to hours and resolve quickly.
- **Episodic**: Stressors recur periodically but allow for recovery in between.
- **Sustained**: Stressors persist continuously over days or weeks without respite.
- **Chronic**: Stressors are present over months with no clear resolution.
- **Accumulating**: Stressors compound over time, building intensity and impact.

## BDI Cycle Reference States

### Intention Clarity States
- **Clear and Actionable**: Intentions are well-formed, specific, and ready for execution.
- **Generally Defined**: Intentions are present but may lack detail or precision.
- **Vague or Uncertain**: Intentions are poorly defined or ambiguous.
- **Conflicted**: Multiple competing intentions create internal tension.
- **Absent or Disrupted**: Intentions are unclear, abandoned, or incoherent.

### Goal Alignment States
- **Fully Aligned**: Individual and collective goals are harmonious and mutually reinforcing.
- **Mostly Aligned**: Goals are generally compatible with minor divergences.
- **Partially Aligned**: Significant areas of misalignment or competing priorities.
- **Conflicted Goals**: Goals are in direct opposition, creating friction.
- **Fragmented Goals**: No coherent goal structure. Efforts are scattered and contradictory.

---

## Summary

These reference states provide a **qualitative vocabulary** for describing crew functioning across:
- **Physiological dimensions** (sleep, stress, capacity)
- **Cognitive dimensions** (clarity, decision-making, understanding)
- **Emotional dimensions** (mood, regulation)
- **Social dimensions** (cohesion, communication, trust)
- **Workload dimensions** (demands, resources, adaptability)
- **Environmental dimensions** (operational pressure, temporal patterns)
- **Intentional dimensions** (goal clarity, alignment)

These states are purely **descriptive and conceptual**, offering a shared language for characterizing crew condition without numeric measurement or computational representation.
